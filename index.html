Call Me Link

Not because it's my legal name — but because the name represents something I've tried to do throughout my life: connect things that often seem disconnected.

By Ben Linkous

My name is Ben Linkous, and my life has been shaped by service, learning, and the ongoing effort to understand how individuals can contribute meaningfully to the world around them.

Until recently, I served in the United States military. Like many veterans, the transition out of service was not as smooth as I expected. Military life provides a sense of structure and mission that can be difficult to replicate once the uniform comes off. The silence of the civilian job market was difficult. It challenged my confidence and forced me to confront a kind of uncertainty that many veterans experience but few speak about openly.

I remain proud of my service — and try to speak about it honestly and without exaggeration. I can't claim I did anything particularly valorous. I fulfilled my responsibilities alongside others who did the same. But I had the privilege of serving with people who embodied courage and dedication in ways that deserve lasting respect. Standing beside them remains one of the greatest honors of my life.

One responsibility that remains deeply important to me is remembering those who did not come home. Service does not truly end when the uniform is set aside. In many ways, the deeper responsibility begins afterward: remembering those who gave everything, and honoring the lives they lived.

Writing has become a way for me to think through the ideas that shape my life — on technology and cybersecurity, media literacy and propaganda, military service and the transition to civilian life, education and leadership, and community and civic responsibility.

The name Link carries a simple meaning. A link connects two points. It bridges distance. It carries information from one place to another. I hope to connect disciplines that are often separated — technology and ethics, service and education, global events and local responsibility.

The Story

Writing from the Middle of It

I'm Ben Linkous — a retired Army Master Sergeant who has lived in Pulaski nearly his entire life. I grew up riding bikes on Lake Street, was a bat boy at Calfee Park, and have watched this town change over decades.

I write because I believe that the most important conversations aren't happening in Washington — they're happening in town halls, on porches, and at Planning Commission meetings where very few people show up.

I serve on the Pulaski Planning Commission. I run NRV Managed IT. I'm building things I hope make a difference. This is where I think out loud about all of it.
